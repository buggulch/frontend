Trona Pinnacles, Surprise Canyon, Red Rock Canyon State Park - Car Camping

Location: Trona Pinnacles, Surprise Canyon, Red Rock Canyon State Park
Type of trip: Car Camping
Date: Apr. 1-3, 2016
People: Max Chacon, Sid Maurer, David Krawitz, Drew Taylor

Five of us left the Cal Poly dorms after class on a Friday and all managed to squeeze into Dave's 2008 Ford Ranger. Three of us in the front, two in the back. We arrived at Red Rock Canyon State Park late in the night and nabbed an empty handicap camp spot. In the morning, we putted around the campground and surrounding trails. Dave found a cigarette cleanly lying on some rocks off a hiking trail. He proclaimed it to be "desert preserved".

We then made our way to the ghost town of Ballarat. The town had a decrepit "store", where a crusty old man sold us some beer out of a cooler. The refreshments fueled our hike up the nearby Surprise Canyon, an oasis tucked in Death Valley's Panamint Mountains. That night we camped beneath the Trona Pinnacles. It was a movie like setting.

On our last day, we hiked up some pinnacles then began the drive back to SLO. Sid had a phone interview he wanted to be back for but it didn't look we would make it in time. He requested we pull off in Creston to wait for the phone call. We vetoed the decision and promised we would pull off when needed. When the call came, there was no pull off. Sid took the interview in the car with the four of us inches away. The call dropped minutes into the interview.
