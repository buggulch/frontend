Death Valley National Park - Car Camping

Location: Death Valley National Park
Type of trip: Car Camping
Date: Dec. 31, 2020 - Jan. 4, 2021
People: Matt Lane, Michael McGill, Sean Yao

This was the San Carlos crew’s 4th annual new years camping trip. Everyone was beyond down for a solid 4 nighter because the previous year’s trip to Sinkyone Wilderness was so good. The Death Valley idea arose because of the projected rain throughout the rest of California.

We left San Carlos on new year’s eve morning and managed to get to the Trona Pinnacles with enough time to hike around, play catch, and toss some die. Winds picked up at sun down but eased up by dark. We had a nice fire and played John Pardi’s Heartache Mediation album fully through.

The next day we cruised into the national park and hiked Fall Canyon. A quarter mile before the end of the main canyon, we stopped to check our guidebook for directions. The book, Hiking Death Valley by Michael Digonnet, noted that at the end of the main canyon there is a “slick 18-foot fall” with a bypass that allows you to get to the canyon’s best narrows. As we reviewed the book, two old guys walked by and one stated, “I see you have the bible”. They went on to praise the book and tell us how they did not make it past the fall but that we most definitely will. The bypass ended up being more difficult than expected but we did make it. The narrows were exceptional and once we reached the end of the tightest section of narrows, we spotted a bighorn sheep on the walls above us. We finished the hike at golden hour and didn’t find a camp spot until dark. Death Valley dispersed campsites can be very hard to come by.

The next couple days we explored Ubehebe Crater, Furnace Creek, Badwater Basin, and Sidewinder Canyon. The Sidewinder Canyon narrows were the highlight of the trip. We left the park through the town of Shoshone and drove 8 hours home on our last day.
